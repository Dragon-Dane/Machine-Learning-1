First Pass (5 to 10 minutes)
1. Title, Abstract, Introduction
2. Section and subsection headings
3. Conclusions
--------------
1. Category: What type of paper is this? A measurement paper? An analysis of an existing system? A description of a research prototype?
2. Context: Which other papers is it related to? Which theoretical bases were used to analyze the problem?
3. Correctness: Do the assumptions appear to be valid?
4. Contributions: What are the paper's main contributions?
5. Clarity: Is the paper well written?

Second Pass (Upto 1H)
1. Figures, diagrams and other illustrations in the paper. Special attention to graphs. 
   Are the axes properly labeled? Are results shown with error bars, so that conclusions are statistically significant? 
   Common mistakes like these will separate rushed, shoddy work from the truly excellent.
2. Mark relevant unread references for further reading (this is a good way to learn more about the background of the paper).

Third Pass (4-5H for beginner, 1H for experienced)
1. Re-creation of actual paper.
2. Identify paper's innovations, hidden failings, assumptions.
3. Jot down ideas for future works.
